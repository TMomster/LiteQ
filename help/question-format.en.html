LiteQ Question Format Guide

This guide explains how to write survey question structures

Syntax Overview

LiteQ uses a simple plain-text format to define survey structures. Keywords are case-insensitive, and colons are optional.

Each keyword goes on its own line, followed by the content (separated by a colon or space).

Keyword Reference

titleSurvey title, appears only once. Required.
desSurvey description, optional. Displayed below the title.
scSingle Choice question, list options below with indentation. At least 2 options.
mcMultiple Choice question, list options below with indentation. At least 2 options.
aOpen-ended question, no options. Displays a text input area on the results page.

Format Rules

  • For sc and mc, list options with indentation (spaces or tabs) below the question
  • Options may be prefixed with a dash - or asterisk *, or no prefix at all
  • Option content cannot be empty
  • Duplicate options within the same question are not allowed
  • Separate questions with blank lines for clarity (not required)
  • a needs no options
  • Questions are numbered automatically as 1, 2, 3...
  • Options are labeled automatically as A, B, C...

Complete Example

title User Satisfaction Survey
des This survey aims to understand user satisfaction with our products

sc What is your gender
  Male
  Female

sc What is your age group
  18-25
  26-35
  36-45
  45+

mc Which of our products have you used
  Product A
  Product B
  Product C
  Product D

a Do you have any suggestions for our products

About the ae Keyword

The ae keyword was previously used for "additional open-ended questions" and has been merged into a. If ae is used, it will be treated as a automatically.

Using with AI

Click the "Copy Full Text" button on the main LiteQ interface to copy the complete syntax guide, format rules, and example to your clipboard.

Paste into an AI model, then write your survey topic and requirements at the end. The AI will generate a survey structure that conforms to LiteQ syntax.

Example: Please generate a survey about commuting habits with 5 questions
